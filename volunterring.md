Volunteering
Nepal is a country of breathtaking beauty, abundant wildlife, and fascinating traditional cultures. This country is known as “Land of Paradise”. Economically, Nepal is based on Agriculture as a huge portion of its population heavily relies on Agriculture. However, the agriculture sector of Nepal faces tremendous challenges regarding quality seeds, fertilizers, modern technologies, sustaining market and agricultural education among others

We are non-governmental organization (NGO) registered with Nepal government and affiliated with Social Welfare Council of Nepal working in the field of Agriculture emphasizing on development of Sustainable Agriculture in Nepal.Our program makes a positive difference in the lives of the deprived society by providing volunteering opportunities to international volunteers.

We provide Nepali culture and training for foreign volunteers who wish to render their services to Nepal in the field of organic agriculture in our organic agriculture resource center and farms. The volunteers can work in other farms which are located in beautiful location. Beside this they can work in the field of Education, Health, Community Development, Orphanage, Disable, Agro -Forestry, Environment and Ecology, Poverty alleviation etc.

We, also make visit to surrounding villages for cultural exchange program as Nepal is widely rich in diverse culture. Nepali culture is distinctively friendly and open and volunteers are made to feel as though they are part of the family. They can enjoy traditional rural villages especially in Gurung/Magar/Darai/Kumal/Bote/Tamang communities.  During this period, volunteers will have the opportunity to participate in local festivals and to learn more about Nepali culture and exchange their culture.

Volunteers also have a chance of adventure, such as white water rafting,  Annapurna trekking and a visit to Royal Chitwan National Park and elephant safari are other attractions. The birth place of Lord Buddha the Lumbini also can be visited in the same time.

The volunteer program is available to individuals or to groups of up to 10 people at a time for the period of one week to five months. We would also like to invite school groups, university students, adult groups and professional organizations who are interested in organic agriculture to create and run custom programs for cultural visits, service projects, volunteer works and internship works in Govardhan Organic Agriculture Resource Center and other farms to dirt their hand working in the farm. Where, volunteers can enjoy diverse culture of different communities and a mixture of natural beauty.

With our extensive local network, experienced staff and comprehensive information about all aspects of Nepal, our staff is able to develop extraordinary opportunities in any corner of Nepal. Our group program is equal in quality to other international groups working in Nepal; please contact us in these ID sadpnepal@gmail.com for more information.

Volunteers/visitors/researchers/students/Interns and other are heartily welcome to join the program with their children, partners or group of friends.

Training and support.

Once volunteers have been accepted for placement, you will receive advice and support throughout your preparations.  Volunteers will get instruction about the culture, food, and behavior of the villages and also will be instructed about local language.

PROGRAM REQUIREMENTS.

Volunteers for the Nepal program must.

Have no major health problems.
Have a positive and outgoing personality.


Program cost:

A US150 application fee (non-refundable) will secure your place on the program, with your program fee due for payment eight weeks before you begin your placement.  You only pay your application fee once and then you can access any of SADP volunteer programs over the next 2 years.

Personal staff support
Your Nepal program guide
Opportunities of a 1-week or more tour around Nepal (it depends on duration of time)
